LIO-HKDT targets the runtime bottleneck of nearest-neighbor search and incremental map update in LiDAR-inertial odometry.

The paper proposes Hash K-D Tree (hkd-Tree), which uses hashed voxel indices to manage lightweight local k-d trees. A voxel distribution mechanism and buffered update strategy further improve KNN search and point-cloud insertion efficiency.

Experiments show that LIO-HKDT maintains accuracy comparable to state-of-the-art LiDAR-inertial odometry systems while significantly improving runtime efficiency.
