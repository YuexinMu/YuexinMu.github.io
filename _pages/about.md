I am a master's student in Software Engineering at Chongqing University. My research focuses on LiDAR-inertial odometry, SLAM, robotic localization, and efficient point-cloud data structures for real-time autonomous systems.

My recent work explores how to make LiDAR-inertial odometry both accurate and fast. I proposed LIO-DPC, a dynamic pose chain framework that combines the real-time performance of filtering methods with the accuracy benefits of graph optimization. I also proposed LIO-HKDT, a LiDAR-inertial odometry system based on a Hash K-D Tree data structure for efficient KNN search and incremental map updates.

Before joining Chongqing University, I received my B.E. in Computer Science and Technology from Guangdong University of Technology. I have broad experience in robotics competitions, ROS-based unmanned vehicle development, SLAM, motion control, and embedded systems.

Research Interests
